# Draconic Language Grammar Rules

## Sentence Structure
Draconic typically follows a Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) structure, similar to English.

## Verbs
- Verbs do not conjugate for person (I, you, he/she/it)
- Tense is indicated by suffixes:
  - Present: -ir (neutral)
  - Past: -is
  - Future: -ith
  - Imperative: -ora

## Nouns
- Plurals are formed with the suffix -ar
- Possessive is indicated with the prefix vi-

## Pronouns
- I/me: wux
- You: svihelen (formal) / svil (informal)
- He/him: rerth
- She/her: vutha
- It: svent
- We/us: wuxar
- They/them: verthar

## Adjectives
- Adjectives follow the noun they modify
- Comparatives use the prefix mhar-
- Superlatives use the prefix thric-

## Numbers
- One: unui
- Two: duil
- Three: thurliost
- Four: crendar
- Five: daoshan
- Six: sennith
- Seven: turthik
- Eight: rekish
- Nine: nuvol
- Ten: dexar

## Common Phrases
- Hello: Thurkear
- Goodbye: Astahir
- Thank you: Virlym
- Please: Tenpiswo
- Yes: Shar
- No: Nishka
